PHILOSOPHY

SIMPLICITY

and elegance was the idea for this design. Much like the school it represents, I wanted the car to represent a sophistication in thought and execution. In a world of immediacy and ease brought about by digital design methods an processes, I felt it important to take a step back in designing the vehicle and approach it with a caution of the “coolness” factor in designing the graphic element for a race car, and avoid begin sucked into making a design which was rooted in sensationalism.

GROWING

up I was exposed to an extensive curriculum of European car culture by my father who in his youth had raced formula 2000’s (Renault 2.0 liter) in France, and later went on to established his own brand (icengineworks) from his passion of motorsports. Needless to say motorsports has been a big part of my life, and embarking on a project such as this one has been a dream of mine since childhood.

CONCEPT

was the most important aspect of this design for me. The structure of everything had to have a sturdy philosophical foundation from which everything would spring. I chose to start with the logo of The Cooper Union setting aside its colors as my palette. Using its structure I created one of my own (fg.1-2) use it a basis on which I would create my design. I created the shape (fg.3) imagining three intersecting strokes, each one diminishing in thickness; rotating and decending in every iteration, inhabiting the entirety of the shape and creating the landscape for a complex, multilateral juxtaposition of color relationship. All the objects implemented are treated as pure abstractions of shape and color, used to create a landscape in which they may interact freely. The numbers are utilized as colors and shapes freeing them from their symbolic signifers and allow them to be viewed as sculptural forms. this relationship and proximity is confounded when viewing the vehicle in motion, as it rotates the colors and shapes interlock in new combinations creating new relationships in the viewers eye.
